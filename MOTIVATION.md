# Motivation: Why Diversity Matters

> **Note**: This documentation was LLM-generated. If something seems wrong or contradicts the code, please report bugs.

This document summarizes Section 2 (Background) from the theoretical paper underpinning this project.

## Why Is Diversity Lost?

- **Archive bias**: Training data encodes historical patterns that underrepresent or misrepresent minoritized populations
- **Mode collapse**: Generative models fail to capture the complete diversity of training data - they preserve dominant modes while erasing rare ones
- **Amplification**: Mode collapse amplifies biases already baked in by societal structures

## Why Is Diversity Important?

- **Rare events matter**: Long tails contain interesting and important realities (catastrophes, innovations, edge cases)
- **Outliers are powerful**: Each represents a possible real mechanism not yet considered
- **Structural inequity**: Some long tails originate from social marginalization - without intervention, GenAI worsens lives of the minoritized
- **Invisibility**: The most ethically important cases are often hard to detect - they escape our classification systems

## Who Does Homogenization Harm?

- **The minoritized**: Amplifies representational, allocational, and narrative harms
- **Everyone else**: The margins are powerful sources of creative production - structural violence never quite stifles this generative energy
- **The surround**: The field beyond surveillance and discipline is impoverished when traces of deviance are erased

## Existential Risks

- **Narrative shaping**: LLMs shape minds and behavior in profound ways (AI-induced psychosis, filter bubbles)
- **Knowledge collapse**: Over time, low diversity shrinks our ability to interpret experience and entertain alternatives
- **Escalation**: Algorithmic homogenization has already contributed to real-world violence and genocide
- **Integration**: Risks compound as AI becomes more powerful and deeply integrated into daily life

## Why Is Diversity Complex?

- **Context-dependence**: Diversity is only meaningful relative to a context (lexical variation vs. gender representation are different things)
- **Unreliable techniques**: Increasing temperature increases incoherence more than novelty
- **Alignment harms diversity**: Current alignment methods actively reduce output diversity
- **Unresolved trade-offs**: How to make AI safe without making it homogenizing remains understudied

## Diverse How, Anyway?

- **Hallucinations are contextual**: What counts as a "defect" is actually a prescription - it depends on task and values
- **Expressive formalisms needed**: Rich productive variation should not be dismissed as mere defect
- **Validity is contextual**: Specifying what counts as valid requires nuance

## What Does Critical Theory Teach Us?

- **Orientation** (Ahmed): We come into every experience oriented - facing a certain way, closer to some things than others
- **Normativity**: The set of orientations power has laid out for us to follow - the default path formed by repetition
- **Queerness**: Deviance from dominant modes - not a specific identity, but a structural condition of moving against the default
- **Reference frames**: Whatever a system treats as the default path, queerness refers to what steers away from it

## What Do We Want?

To promote diversity, GenAI should:

- **Be queer**: Diverge into the long tails
- **Center the subaltern**: Attend to traces rendered invisible by power
- **Be explicit**: Specify what diversity means in context
